Mikado Pheasant

National Science Museum

Introduction

Mikado Pheasant is known as “emperor’s pheasant” because it was named after the Japanese emperor.And its nickname is King of the mist.It is a species of pheasant endemic to Taiwan.

圖片來源:謝郁震

圖片來源:中央社

Habitat

They live in high elevation mountainous areas in Taiwan like Mount Dasyue.And they prefer dense shrubs and steep slopes.

Description

The description of males are dark blue plumage feather and white stripes on its wings and tail.Females are brown plumage to camouflage.

Behavior

Mikado Pheasant comes out to search for food when rainy,foggy and misty.They mostly appear at dawn and dusk.They usually solitary or in pairs.They roost in trees.

Mating

They have potential mate, and they usually mate in March to June.They perform a wing-whirring with a signature move.

圖片來源:黃源明

圖片來源:邱文強

Breeding

Their breeding season is between April to August.Both sexes perform a wing-whirring display.
Females can lay three to five eggs per brood, and they gestation for 23 days.

Feeding

Mikado Pheasants’ feeding habit is similar to chickens.They walk while foraging for food.And they make “clucking” sounds while eating.Their diet is fruit, leaves, vegetation, seeds and insects.

Uniqueness

You can see them on Taiwan’s NT1000 banknote.Besides,they are Endemic species in Taiwan.

Conservation

There are some threats to Mikado Pheasants like natural disasters such as typhoons, mudslides and human hunted them for their meat and feathers.
