Giant Squid

National Science Museum

Introduction

In the science museum, one of the most captivating exhibits is the “Giant Squid”.
It is female and is nine meters long (about three story building).It is from New Zealand.It died at the age of two.
Its natural enemy is sperm whales, and there are two reasons.The first reason is scars of the sucker rings on sperm whales.The second reason is the discovery of beaks from giant squids in the stomachs of sperm whales.
We know little about giant squid because they live in deep oceans (500m~1000m).

圖片來源:The Australian Museum

Squid

Squid’s size is 3cm to 18m.It has a triangular shaped head.And has two fins on their heads.Its limbs have eight arms, two tentacles with hooks or sucker rings.It has a small soft shell inside the body. To defend itself, it can squeeze out ink.It live in the deep open ocean.It can use fins when moving slow.It shoot out water to propel itself while moving quickly.It use two feeding tentacles to hunt.

Octopus

Octopus’s size is 1cm to 9m.It has a round head.Its limbs have eight arms with hooks or sucker rings.It has a completely soft shell.It is masters of camouflage like changing colors, skin shape or skin texture to defend itself, but it is colorblind.It settle on the ocean floor.It isn’t good swimmer.It walk or crawl along the ocean floor.It use arms to capture prey, inject poison to cause paralysis, and then release salivary enzyme to loosen meat.
