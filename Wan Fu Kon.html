Wan Fu Kon

National Science Museum

Introduction

Wan Fu Kon is the temple which worshiped for Patrol Gods (Wufu Chienshui).They protect people from adversity and bad luck.Wan is meaning ten thousand.Fu is luck.And Kon is temple.

Wan Fu Kon has three parts.
The first is called Worshiping Hall.
You can face outside to worship for The Emperor of Universe. The animal with a dragon head and a fish body is called the ridge poles of roofs, which can swallow all evil influences like fire.
The second is called San Chun Hall.The right side is the door of dragons for worshiper to enter.The life side is tiger door for worshiper to leave. The middle door is reserved for deities.On the ceiling, there is a caisson ceiling which is like spider web.The color around it is darker because of the smoke ash.
The third is called The Grand Hall. You can see rats with pumpkins which symbolize fertility and productivity.Peony in a vase means safe and wealthy.The last one are magpie and plum blossom, which stands for overjoyed.

When you are in a temple, you may see some people use moon blocks which are called jiaobei.Moon blocks should be used in a designated area. How to use moon blocks?
First, take a pair of moon blocks.Second, ask the gods a yes-no question, then toss them onto the floor.Last, you can know the gods’ answer.
