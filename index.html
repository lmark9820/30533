Museum Guided Tour

National Science Museum

Introduction

Chungming Senior High School is close to the National Science Museum, so it has organized a special lesson “Museum Guided Tour”.In this lesson, we can learn seven exhibits of various cultures or creatures in English.During this lesson, my vocabulary and my English speaking skill have progressed.

The most impressive part

The most impressive part is the mollusks observation in the laboratory.This activity was combined in giant squid exhibit and the mollusks.In this lesson, we can learn how to distinguish the mollusks and touch their the textures.It was a fascinating and cool experience for me because it was the first time that I touched them.And I knew more knowledge of them.

The most interesting part

After finishing this lesson, I became very interested in a special exhibition “Living With Fire”.Because fire is frequently used in everyday life, I want to get more knowledge of fire.
Humans use fire in many places.

There are generally three primary purposes:cooking food, illumination, gunpowder, and firearms.
There are two methods to cook food.First is stone boiling, people put a hot stone into a pot and make water boil and food well-cooked.The other is smoking, which means drying and dehydrating food to extend the food’s preservation time. People use torches or oil lamps and candles to active at night and live in caves.
Gunpowder and firearms let people hunt for creatures.

How to make fire?
There are two methods.One is “drilling and friction to make fire”.Use a strip of wood to drill quickly with another wood.When it reaches a high temperature, put fiber there. And you can make fire. The other method is “striking rocks to make fire”.Use quartz tap each other to make fire.And quartz can be used many times.

How to preserve fire?
Because making fire was very difficult at that time, humans need to preserve fire.They use a tinder container, which is preserved fire in a low-oxygen to burn slowly.If you want to use fire, you can open a tinder container to allow fire contact air to reignite.
